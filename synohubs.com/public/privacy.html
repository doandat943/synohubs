Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 9, 2026

1. Overview

SynoHub ("the App") is a mobile application that lets you manage your Synology NAS devices. We are committed to protecting your privacy. This policy describes what data we collect, how it's used, and your rights.

2. Data We Do NOT Collect

SynoHub is built with a privacy-first architecture. We do NOT:

  • Collect or store your NAS credentials on our servers
  • Use analytics, tracking, or telemetry
  • Transmit your files, photos, or media through our servers
  • Access your NAS on your behalf
  • Sell or share any user data with third parties

3. Data Stored Locally on Your Device

The following data is stored only on your device using encrypted storage (AES-256):

  • NAS connection profiles (host, port, username, password)
  • App preferences and settings
  • Session tokens for active NAS connections

This data never leaves your device unless you explicitly choose to back it up.

4. Google Drive Backup (Optional)

If you opt to use Google Drive Backup:

  • Your NAS profiles are AES-256 encrypted before uploading
  • Data is stored in your personal Google Drive appDataFolder — a hidden, app-specific folder
  • Only the SynoHub app on your device can decrypt and read this data
  • We do NOT have access to your Google Drive

5. Google Authentication

SynoHub uses Google Sign-In for the following purposes only:

  • Identity verification — to identify your account for VIP membership
  • Drive app data scope — to read/write encrypted backups to your own Google Drive

We request minimal OAuth scopes: email and drive.appdata. We do NOT access your Google Drive files, contacts, or any other personal data.

6. NAS Connection

SynoHub connects directly to your Synology NAS via:

  • Local Area Network (LAN)
  • Your own domain name or DDNS
  • QuickConnect (Synology's own relay service)

No traffic is routed through SynoHub servers. We do not operate relay servers or proxy services.

7. VIP Membership

When you purchase VIP membership:

  • Your email address is stored in Firebase Firestore to verify VIP status
  • No payment information is stored by SynoHub — payments are processed by Google Play or the payment provider

8. Third-Party Services

  • TMDB (The Movie Database) — used for media metadata and poster images. No user data is sent to TMDB.
  • Firebase — used only for VIP membership verification via Firestore.
  • Google Sign-In — for authentication (email + Drive app data scope only).

9. Children's Privacy

SynoHub is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated date.
